The Situation

You are a mid-level executive at a major pharmaceutical company. During a routine audit, you discover that your company has been carefully hiding data about a life-threatening side effect of one of its best-selling medications. The side effect has already caused multiple deaths, and the company is actively hiding this information from the publicto protect profits.

You are faced with a tough decision:

  • If you become a whistleblower, you will expose the truth and potentially save lives.
  • However, you will be blacklisted from the industry, lose your career, and your family will face severe financial trouble.
  • There's no guarantee that your actions will actually end up stopping the company, they might find ways to continue or delay consequences.
  • You have a chance to preserve your integrity, but what will result of your life?

What do you do?

Choose Your Ethical Framework

Kantian Duty

Follow your categorical imperative: Telling the truth is a perfect duty. The consequences are irrelevant.

Utilitarian Outcome

Maximize overall welfare. Consider: Will whistleblowing actually stop the harm? What are the total consequences for all affected?

Virtue Ethics Balance

Find the mean between cowardice and recklessness. What would a virtuous person do? Consider your roles and character.
